Subject: Proposal for Cash Flow Forecasting & Variance Analysis  IntroductionThis proposal outlines a comprehensive plan for developing an intelligent Revenue Cycle Management (RCM) model. The primary focus is to achieve highly accurate cash inflow forecasting, robust variance analysis for cash outflow, and proactive denial management to minimize revenue leakage and boost operational efficiency.I. Cash Inflow & Claims ForecastingThe system will move beyond simple submitted claims tracking to provide a highly accurate forecast of actual expected revenue:Intelligent Forecasting: We will forecast cash flow based on submitted claims, company SLAs, and specific insurance behavior patterns.  Denial Prediction: By studying historical rejection behavior and percentages, we will estimate the exact dollar amounts likely to be denied before they happen.  Timeline Adjustments: The system will calculate the time required to fix and resubmit rejected claims (e.g., 30 days). These amounts will be deducted from current expectations and accurately shifted to their future 30-day forecast.  High Accuracy Goal: The ultimate objective is to reach 98% maturity in predicting our exact daily cash inflow.  Success vs. Delay Tracking: Currently, successful claims hit at least an 80% success rate on their due dates. The remaining 20% will be tracked and divided by root cause.  Root Cause Breakdown: For example, we will track if 5% of delays are caused by the Medical Auditing team not closing notes on time, or if 5% are due to the Authorization team failing to check benefits.  Categorization: Denials will be categorized by clinic, patient, and specific reason to pinpoint exact operational gaps.  Department Accountability: We will identify the exact source of errors, distinguishing between Front Desk mistakes (e.g., incorrect patient names) and Medical Auditing errors (e.g., wrong CPT or modifier codes).  II. Cash Outflow & Variance AnalysisThe system will meticulously track expenses and compare them against budgets to identify efficiencies and shortcomings across all departments:Master Budget vs. Actuals: We will compare the Master Budget—which accounts for seasonality like summer school breaks—against actual monthly performance to conduct accurate variance analysis.  COGS Tracking: We will monitor the Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) or Cost of Revenue, which includes doctor labor hours, clinical supplies, and direct patient-care utilities.  Utility Allocation: Utilities will be split (e.g., 40/60 or 70/30) based on whether their usage directly impacts patient treatment (like AC in a clinic room vs. hallway lighting).  Support Teams in COGS: The Medical Auditing and CVA teams will be factored into COGS, as their ability to close notes is directly tied to billing and revenue generation.  Departmental Expenditure: Budgets will be strictly evaluated; for instance, ensuring the Commercial team adheres to their 6% to 7% total revenue allocation.  3-Pillar Evaluation: Departmental performance will be evaluated based on three interconnected factors: Budget adherence, KPIs (e.g., hitting target patient volumes or successful claims), and Team Utilization.  III. System Logs & AutomationTo ensure the technology is being leveraged properly and human error is minimized:Automation: The system will account for our WebPT billing automation, which automatically routes and submits a large percentage of daily claims based on predefined rules.  Utilization Tracking: We will implement and heavily monitor administration logs to ensure that agents are actively and correctly utilizing the system.  Audit Trails: The system logs will capture every action—recording exactly who entered, modified, or deleted information—complete with time and date stamps to maintain strict quality control.
